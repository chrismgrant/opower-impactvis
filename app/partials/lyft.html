At Lyft, my work consisted of front-end development (html, css, AngularJS) and often visual/layout design for lyft.me, emails for passengers and drivers, driver support tools, and a redesign for RegionCfg, a tool used for configuring and/or updating Lyft cities.

Lyft Driver Mentoring Flow

Lyft actively recruits and trains new drivers through a driver mentoring program. In the mentoring program, a recruit is mentored by a seasoned driver. To attract get more drivers to become mentors, a front-end wizard on lyft.me needed to be designed and engineered. I was tasked with the front-end engineering along with visual and layout design of the mentoring wizard for desktop and mobile clients.

Lyft RegionCfg

Processes involving pricing, coverage, and other vital information regarding cities is done through the region configuration tool. V1 of this tool suffered from a variety of usability issues. The system relied on a high level of learnability — forms weren’t meaningful or understandable to most users, users had to memorize types of input required for fields, and users had difficulty evaluating their actions. Region Config v1 essentially created a gulf of evaluation and execution for users. The goals of the redesign were to make the user experience clear and understandable, easily readable, and reviewable. Where possible, I added more dynamic features to make the use of this tool easier.
